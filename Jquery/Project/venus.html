Venus

Venus is the second planet from the sun and is the closest planet to Earth. It is often referred to as Earth's sister planet because of its similar size, composition, and proximity.

Some Intersting Facts About Venus
  • Venus has a thick atmosphere made up of mostly carbon dioxide. This atmosphere is so dense that it creates a strong greenhouse effect, trapping heat and making Venus the hottest planet in our solar system, with surface temperatures that can reach up to 864 degrees Fahrenheit (462 degrees Celsius).
  • The surface of Venus is covered in volcanic features, including vast lava plains, towering shield volcanoes, and deep canyons. The planet also has a thick layer of clouds that are made up of sulfuric acid
  • Venus rotates very slowly, taking about 243 Earth days to complete one rotation. However, it takes only about 225 Earth days to orbit the sun. This means that a day on Venus (one complete rotation) is actually longer than a year on Venus (one orbit around the sun).
  • Venus is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty, and its symbol is a circle with a cross, representing the goddess's hand mirror.
  • Venus is visible to the naked eye from Earth and is often referred to as the "morning star" or the "evening star" depending on its position in the sky.
